About the Project

Voting in Crisis: Analyzing the Impact of COVID-19 on the 2020 Election in New York State

Welcome

Image 1 Description

The 2020 election unfolded during the unprecedented COVID-19 pandemic, which profoundly affected voting behavior nationwide. In New York State, home to over 19.3 million residents, the pandemic had a dramatic impact. Over 1.5 million COVID-19 cases and more than 35,000 deaths were reported by the end of 2020, creating a public health crisis that discouraged in-person voting and shifted the focus toward absentee ballots. In the 2020 general election, New York saw a record 45% increase in absentee ballot usage compared to previous years, with more than 2 million absentee ballots cast out of a total 8.6 million votes.

Our team seeks to analyze how these extraordinary circumstances shaped voter turnout, voting methods, and participation trends across New York State. By integrating demographic data, COVID-19 statistics, and election results, we aim to uncover how New Yorkers adapted to these challenges and what it reveals about the resilience of democracy in the face of crisis. Explore our website to learn more about the intersection of public health and electoral processes during one of the most transformative elections in history.

Motivation

Meet Our Team

Thank you so much for visiting our site! We are a team of graduate students at Columbia University’s Mailman School of Public Health, united by a shared passion for understanding the intersections of public health, data science, and democracy. Coming from diverse academic backgrounds and programs, we’ve joined forces to analyze the impact of COVID-19 on voting behavior during the historic 2020 election in New York State.

Through this project, we aim to sharpen our skills in applied biostatistics, data visualization, and public health communication while contributing meaningful insights into how crises shape electoral processes. We hope our work not only informs but also inspires conversations about the resilience of democratic systems in the face of unprecedented challenges.

About Our data

Data Dictionary

Demographic and Voting Data

state: Name of the U.S. state for the corresponding data.

county: Name of the county in New York State.

age_group: Categorized age groups of voters (“18-24”, “25-44”, “45-64”, “65+”).

gender: Gender of voters, either “Male” or “Female.”

race: Racial demographics of voters (“White”, “Black”, “Asian”, “Hispanic”, “Other”). education_level: Education attainment level of voters (“Less than High School”, “High School Graduate”, “Some College”, “Bachelor’s Degree or Higher”).

income_bracket: Income level of voters categorized by brackets (“<$25,000”, “$25,000-$50,000”, “$50,000-$75,000”, “>$75,000”).

voter_turnout: Total number of voters who participated in the election within the demographic group.

total_population: Total population of the demographic group.

percent_turnout: Percentage of voters who participated in the election relative to the total population of the demographic group.

margin_of_error: Statistical uncertainty for turnout estimates.

Socioeconomic Indicators

poverty_rate: Percentage of the population living below the poverty line.

median_household_income: Median income of households in the state.

unemployment_rate: Percentage of the labor force that is unemployed.

gini_index: Measure of income inequality within the state (0 = perfect equality, 1 = maximum inequality).

healthcare_access_rate: Percentage of residents with access to healthcare services.

Health Facilities

facility_name: Name of the health facility.

facility_type: Type of the facility (value is either “Hospital,” “Clinic” or “Urgent Care”).

latitude: Latitude coordinate of the facility’s location.

longitude: Longitude coordinate of the facility’s location.

bed_count: Total number of beds available at the facility.

covid_capacity: Capacity for treating COVID-19 patients.

staff_count: Total number of healthcare staff employed.

Election Results

candidate: Name of the candidate in the 2020 election.

party: Political party of the candidate (values is either “Democrat” or “Republican”).

votes: Total number of votes received by the candidate.

percent_votes: Percentage of total votes received by the candidate in the county.

absentee_votes: Total number of absentee votes received by the candidate.

in_person_votes: Total number of in-person votes received by the candidate.

COVID-19 Data

cases: Total number of confirmed COVID-19 cases in 2020.

deaths: Total number of COVID-19-related deaths in 2020.

cases_per_100k: COVID-19 cases per 100,000 residents.

deaths_per_100k: COVID-19 deaths per 100,000 residents.

testing_rate: Number of COVID-19 tests conducted per 100,000 residents.
